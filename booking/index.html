The honest difference

Your page vs a marketplace app

Marketplace booking apps are convenient — but they put your shop in a directory next to everyone else, and your loyal client can leave your booking screen looking at a competitor. Chair is just you.

💈 Chair (your page)

  • ✓Only your shop. Clients never get shown another barber.
  • ✓Keep 100%. No commission skimmed off each booking.
  • ✓Your brand & link. It looks like you, not like an app.
  • ✓One system. Booking + loyalty + referrals together.

🗂️ Marketplace apps

  • ✕A directory. Your clients can browse competitors right next to you.
  • ✕Fees add up. Subscriptions and per-booking or "boost" charges.
  • ✕Their brand. Clients remember the app, not your shop.
  • ✕Separate tools. Loyalty and referrals live somewhere else.

We're not knocking any specific app — marketplaces are a fair trade for discovery. But once clients already know you, a directory works against you. Chair is built for the clients you've already earned.

Questions

Straight answers

How is this different from Booksy or other booking apps?

Chair is your own branded booking page, not a marketplace. Marketplace apps list your shop in a directory alongside other providers your clients can switch to. Chair only ever shows your shop, and you keep 100% of every booking.

Do my clients need to download an app?

No. They tap your link or scan your QR code and book in seconds in their browser. Nothing to install.

Does it cut down no-shows?

Yes. Clients get an automatic confirmation and reminder, and you can require a deposit for the appointment types that need one.

Can it work with loyalty and referrals?

Yes — Chair is part of the same system as Loop (loyalty) and Ambassadors (referrals), so booking, rebooking, rewards and referrals all work together instead of being separate apps.
