08Before hiring a remodeling contractor

Seven questions to ask anyone you're considering.

Including us. A good contractor will welcome every one of these. If someone dodges them, that tells you something too.

Are you registered and insured?Ask for the PA HIC number and proof of insurance. Both should come out without hesitation.
Who will actually be in my home?Know who's on site day to day, and who your one point of contact is.
How do you handle surprises and changes?Changes should be priced and approved in writing before work continues — never sprung on you after.
What does the payment schedule look like?Payments should track progress. Be careful with anyone who wants most of the money up front.
How will you protect my home day to day?Dust containment, floor protection, and end-of-day cleanup should be part of the plan — not a favor.
How often will I hear from you?You should know the communication rhythm before work starts — and never have to chase for updates.
What happens if I need time to decide?The answer should always be: take it. Pressure to sign today is the biggest red flag there is.

Ask us all seven at your consultation — we'd genuinely enjoy it.

09Common questions

Asked often, answered plainly.

Is the consultation really free — what's the catch?

There's no catch. It's genuinely free and carries no obligation. It's a conversation about your space, your options, and honest cost and timeline expectations — not a sales visit. You'll never be asked to decide on the spot.

Will I get pricing at the consultation?

We'll talk honestly about realistic cost ranges and what drives them — materials, layout changes, plumbing, the age of your home. If you'd like to go further, we follow up with a clear written proposal, explained in plain language.

Can we live in our home during the remodel?

Usually, yes — most of our clients do. We plan around your household: dust containment, floor protection, daily cleanup, and a workable kitchen or bathroom plan while yours is out of service. You'll know exactly what to expect before anything starts.

How long does a remodel usually take?

It depends on the room and the scope, and we'd rather give you a real answer for your home than a generic one. What we can promise: you'll have an honest timeline before you commit, and we'll tell you promptly if anything changes it.

Do you handle the plumbing in basement remodels?

Yes — plumbing-related basement projects are one of our three specialties, alongside kitchens and bathrooms. Bathrooms, wet bars, laundry, drainage — we'll walk the space with you and explain what's possible.

What areas do you serve?

Philadelphia (rowhomes and condos), Chester County (West Chester, Downingtown, Exton, Malvern), and Montgomery County along the Route 422 corridor (Phoenixville, Royersford, Collegeville, Limerick).
