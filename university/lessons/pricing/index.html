Lesson 04 · free

Price it right — keep them forever

Landing a client is step one. The business is in turning that one client into recurring income — and making sure they never want to leave.

Beginners undercharge, do one-off gigs, and stay broke. Here's how to price like a pro and build income that shows up every month.

Pricing

Charge for the result, not the hours

Never price by time — you'll cap yourself and race to the bottom. Price by the outcome: more customers. A shop that gets 10 extra jobs a month doesn't care if it took you 2 hours or 20.

Weak: "$25/hour for social media." Strong: "$500/month to run your content + loyalty and keep customers coming back."
The model

Retainers > one-offs

A one-off is a paycheck. A retainer is a business. Charge a set monthly fee and deliver ongoing. Just a handful of clients on retainer = real, predictable income.

4 clients × $500/mo = $2,000/mo recurring — and it compounds as you add more without losing the old ones.
Proof

Show the results — every month

What gets shown gets renewed. Send a simple monthly recap: new leads, reviews collected, repeat visits, content posted. When they can see the value, cancelling feels like a loss.

The CRM + analytics in the toolstack track this for you — you just screenshot it and send.
Retention

Make them un-leavable with loyalty

Here's the secret weapon: run a loyalty program for their customers (that's what Loop is). Their customers come back more → their revenue climbs → your results look incredible → they'd never fire the person driving that.

You're not just "the content person." You're the reason their regulars keep showing up. That's a client for years.
Grow

Ask for referrals — and raise your rates

Happy, results-getting clients are your best salespeople. Ask: "Who else do you know that could use this?" And as your results stack up, raise your prices for new clients. You're worth more now — charge it.
