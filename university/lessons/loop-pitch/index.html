Lesson 06 · free

The Loop pitch — the easiest local sale

Content gets you in the door. A loyalty program keeps you there. This is the offer that's easiest to say yes to, easiest to prove, and hardest to cancel — which is exactly why it's your wedge.

Every local owner already understands loyalty — the punch card in their drawer proves it. You're not selling a new idea. You're selling the modern, trackable version, done for them.

Why it's easy

It sells a fear they already have

Owners don't lie awake about "brand awareness." They lie awake about regulars who stopped coming. A loyalty program speaks to the pain they already feel: keep the customers you already paid to get. Retention is a far easier yes than "let me get you new customers."

New customers = a promise. Repeat customers = a fix for a problem they can feel. Always lead with the fix.
The one-liner

Pitch it in a single sentence

Don't explain the software. Sell the outcome and let Loop be the how.

"I set up a digital rewards card for your customers — they scan a code, earn visits, and get pulled back in automatically. You keep more regulars without lifting a finger. Want me to set one up for your shop this week?"
The demo

Show it live in 60 seconds

Nothing closes like watching it work. Pull up the demo card on your phone, tap a visit, show the reward unlock, show the owner dashboard filling in. It's real, it's theirs in minutes, and it makes you look like a pro.

Open the demo card and the owner view right in front of them. "This is exactly what yours looks like — I just put your name on it."
Setup

Stand one up in minutes

This is the part that makes you look magic. You create their program, hand them a printable poster for the counter and a staff console, and they're live. No app for customers to download — just a scan.

Their kit: the customer card link, the counter poster, and the staff console to punch + redeem. That's a whole loyalty system, handed over same day.
The lock-in

Why it makes you un-fireable

Here's the strategic part. Once Loop is running, their regulars come back more, their revenue climbs, and you have the dashboard that proves it. Firing you now means turning off the thing driving repeat business. You've gone from "the content person" to "the reason customers keep showing up."

Bundle it: content to bring people in (Lessons 3), Loop to keep them (this one). That combo is a retainer that renews itself.
