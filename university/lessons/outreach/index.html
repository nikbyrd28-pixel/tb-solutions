Lesson 05 · free

Outreach mastery — get the meeting

You've got a niche, an offer, and content. None of it matters until an owner replies. Outreach isn't about being pushy — it's about leading with value and following up when everyone else quits.

Most beginners send three messages, hear nothing, and decide "it doesn't work." The truth: they quit right before the part that works. Here's the whole play.

Mindset

Lead with the gift, not the ask

Nobody replies to "can I do your marketing?" Everybody replies to "I made you something." Do the work first — a reel, a thumbnail, a quick audit — and outreach stops being cold. You're not asking for a chance; you're showing one.

This is the Lesson 1 cheat code made into a habit: make first, pitch second.
Where

Go where owners actually are

Skip the gatekept inbox. Owners answer their own Instagram DMs, their Google Business messages, and — the one nobody uses — their own front door.

Instagram DM — they run the account themselves. In person — walk in at a slow hour with something finished. Referral intro — one happy client's "you should talk to my friend" beats 100 cold messages.
The message

The 4-line opener that gets replies

Short, specific, about them, one easy yes. Long pitches get ignored.

1. Name + genuine specific ("Hey Marco — the reels on your shop's page are underrated.")
2. The gift ("I made you one this morning, no strings.")
3. The proof-drop (attach it or link it.)
4. One tiny ask ("Want three more like it? 10-min call this week?")
In person

The walk-in play

The highest-converting outreach there is, and the one beginners are scared of. Walk in at a dead hour, ask for the owner by name, show the thing on your phone, leave a card. You're a local who made them something — not a salesperson.

Script: "Hi, is Marco around? I'm local, I make short videos for barbershops — I actually made you one, mind if I show you real quick?" Then let the work talk.
The part that closes

Follow up — 5 times, kindly

No reply almost never means no. It means busy. The money is in the follow-up nobody sends. Space them out, stay warm, add a little value each time, then bow out gracefully.

Day 2: "Bumping this up — here's a second one." Day 5: a quick tip, no ask. Day 9: "Should I stop sending these? 😄" Day 14: the polite close — "Door's open whenever." Most yeses land on messages 3–5.
Volume

Make it a number, not a mood

Outreach fails when it's a feeling ("I should reach out sometime"). It works when it's a target. Ten real touches a day — a mix of DMs, one walk-in, one follow-up — and the math from Lesson 2 does the rest. You need four yeses, not four hundred.
