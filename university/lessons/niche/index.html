Pick a niche you can win

The #1 thing that keeps beginners stuck is trying to help everyone. The fastest path to a paying client is picking one market — and getting known for it.

A "niche" just means the type of business you help. A barber. A gym. A roofer. When you pick one, everything gets easier — your pitch, your proof, your referrals. Here's how to choose.

Why it works

Specialists win — and charge more

Nobody hires "a marketer." They hire "the person who does barbershops." Niching makes your outreach sharper, your samples reusable, and your referrals compound — because every client knows five others just like them.

The filter

Money × gap × volume

Pick a niche where all three are true. If one's missing, keep looking.

① Money — one customer is worth $100+ to them (so your results actually matter). ② Gap — they're bad at marketing (so you stand out fast). ③ Volume — there are lots of them near you (so you never run out of prospects).
How to choose

Start with your unfair access

Don't overthink it. Look at what you already have a way into:

• A friend or family member who owns a business? Start there.
• Walk your town's main street — what's everywhere? (salons, restaurants, gyms, contractors)
• Pick something you'd genuinely enjoy talking about every day — you'll make content about it for months.

You're not marrying it. You're picking a starting point. Get one win, then decide if you expand or double down.
The math

Make it feel real

This is where it clicks. Say you charge a shop $500/month to run their content + loyalty. You don't need 100 clients. You need four to replace a full-time income — and each one refers more.

Suddenly "start a business" isn't scary. It's a countable number of conversations away.
Do it now

Commit to one — today

Write it down: "I help ______ get more customers." That one sentence is the foundation for everything — your offer, your outreach, your content. Don't move on until you've filled the blank.
