Part 1

The niche cheat-sheet

Don't serve everyone. Pick one type of local business — you'll get good faster, your outreach gets sharper, and referrals compound.

The filter — pick a niche where all 3 are true: ① one customer is worth $100+ to them · ② they're bad at marketing · ③ there are lots of them near you.
  • 💈 Barbers & salons — repeat customers, super visual (content gold), rarely market well.
  • 💅 Med-spas & aesthetics — high ticket, image-driven, competitive → they'll pay to stand out.
  • 🏋️ Gyms & studios — memberships = recurring revenue, community = endless content.
  • 🦷 Dentists & orthodontists — high customer value, trust-driven (reviews + content win).
  • 🧰 Home services (roofers, HVAC, plumbers, landscapers) — huge job value, terrible marketing, hungry for leads.
  • 🍽️ Restaurants & cafés — foot traffic + loyalty programs crush here (that's what Loop is for).
  • 🚗 Auto (detailing, repair, dealers) — visual, high ticket, love before/after content.
  • 🏡 Realtors — personal brand + content directly wins listings.

Your move: pick ONE today. Get results. Expand later — never before.

Part 2

Outreach scripts

Every one leads with value first. You're not begging for a meeting — you're handing over something good. Swap [name] and [business] and go.

① Instagram DM — value first
"Hey [name] — I actually made your shop a quick promo video (no charge, just thought your content deserved better). Want me to send it over?"
② Walk-in opener
"Hi, I'm [name] — I help [niche] around here get more customers with content and loyalty programs. I made a quick sample for you — mind if I show you 30 seconds?"
③ Follow-up text/email
"Hey [name], following up — here's that promo I made for [business]: [link]. If you like it, I've got a simple way to keep your customers coming back too. Worth a quick chat this week?"
④ The graceful no-reply
"No worries if now's not the time — I'll leave the video with you to use free either way. If you ever want more, I'm one text away. 🙌"
Make the sample in the free Content Studio in minutes — a promo video or a few thumbnails. Walking in with finished work beats any pitch.
Part 3

The call framework

It closes because you're solving their problem — not selling yours. Follow the order. Talk less than they do.

  1. Set the frame."Thanks for the time — I'll keep this quick. Cool if I ask a couple questions first, then show you what I'd do?"
  2. Find the pain."What's the biggest thing holding back new customers right now?" — then shut up and let them talk.
  3. Amplify it."If that kept going another year, what's it costing you?" Make the problem real before you offer the fix.
  4. Bridge to the fix."Here's exactly how I'd solve that…" — tie your offer directly to the pain they just named.
  5. Offer + risk reversal."First month — if you don't love it, you don't pay." Kill their risk and the yes gets easy.
  6. Price, then silence.State the price. Then stop talking. Whoever speaks first loses. Let them respond.
  7. Handle "I'll think about it.""Totally — what specifically do you want to think through?" Uncover the real objection and answer it.
  8. Close."Want to start this week or next?" An easy either/or beats a scary yes/no.
