Most people overthink this. You don't need a website, a logo, or a following. You need one niche, one offer, and the guts to reach out. Here's how — in order.

Step 01

Pick a niche you can actually win

Don't try to serve everyone. Pick one type of local business that (a) makes real money per customer and (b) is bad at marketing. That combo means one new client is worth a lot to them — and you'll stand out fast.

Good picks: barbers & salons, med-spas, gyms, dentists, home services (roofers, HVAC, landscapers), restaurants. Rule: if one customer is worth $100+ to them, keep going.
Step 02

Build one no-brainer offer

Never sell "marketing." Sell a specific result with low risk for them. The clearer the outcome and the safer the yes, the faster they say it.

Weak: "I do social media management." Strong: "I'll get you more booked jobs with content + a loyalty program — first month, if you don't love it, you don't pay."
Step 03

Create proof before you have clients

This is the AI cheat code. Pick a real local business in your niche and make them a free sample — a promo video, a few posts, a loyalty-card demo — using the toolstack. Now you're not "a beginner." You're the person who already made them something.

Make it in the free Content Studio in minutes. Walking in with finished work beats any pitch deck.
Step 04

Reach out by leading with value

Don't ask for a meeting. Give first. Send the free sample, or walk in and hand it over. Giving before asking disarms them and starts the conversation on your terms.

Opener: "Hey — I made your shop a quick promo video, no charge. Thought you deserved better content than what's out there. Want it?" (Full scripts in the free toolkit above.)
Step 05

Close on a simple call

Don't pitch. Ask what their biggest problem getting customers is — then show how your offer fixes exactly that. Give the price. Then stop talking and let them decide.

The complete call framework — every question, in order — is in the free toolkit. It closes without being pushy because you're solving their problem, not selling yours.

Then you deliver — and AI does the heavy lifting. Content Studio for their videos and posts, Loop to keep customers coming back, reviews to build trust, CRM + automations so no lead slips. You run the machine; the software does the work. That's the whole model — and it's what you get, in full, inside TB University.
